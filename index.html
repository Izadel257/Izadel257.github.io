Hi, I'm Paul Claudel Izabayo

I’m a data-driven developer and researcher with a passion for building technologies that are not only functional, but meaningful. I was born and raised in rural Burundi, where access to digital tools was limited — I didn't use a computer until I graduated high school. That experience shaped how I think about equity in tech and continues to guide the projects I work on.

Today, I work at the intersection of data science, community empowerment, and ethical innovation. From analyzing media framing as a Lead Data Scientist at Carleton College’s DataSquad, to co-founding Space-Ed Africa to expand access to tech education in underserved areas, my work is about leveraging technology to build opportunity.

I’m especially interested in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and inclusive data systems. My goal is to create tools that uplift communities — not just observe them. This site showcases some of the public and private projects I’ve contributed to, as well as the journey I’m still on.
